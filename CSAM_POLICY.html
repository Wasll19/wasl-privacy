This policy describes how we address child sexual abuse material (CSAM), sexual exploitation of children, and related harm on the Service. We publish it so users, partners, and regulators can understand our stance and processes.

Legal notice: This document is not legal advice. You should have counsel review it for your jurisdiction(s), business model, and data practices.

1. Our commitment

We have zero tolerance for:

  • CSAM (any visual depiction of sexually explicit conduct involving a minor, as defined by applicable law).
  • Solicitation, grooming, or sexualization of minors.
  • Trading, requesting, or directing others to obtain CSAM or sexual content involving minors.

The Service must not be used to harm children or to facilitate abuse.

3. Prohibited conduct (non-exhaustive)

Users may not:

  • Upload, stream, link to, or otherwise make available CSAM or content that sexualizes minors.
  • Engage in sexual conversations with or directed at minors, or facilitate such contact.
  • Groom, coerce, or manipulate any person for sexual purposes, especially when minors may be involved.
  • Use the Service to arrange real-world harm, trafficking, or sexual exploitation.

We may take action—including immediate termination, preservation of records, and reports to authorities—when we become aware of violations.

6. Law enforcement & specialized organizations

Where we have a good-faith belief that a child is at risk or that CSAM is involved, we will:

  • Preserve relevant information as required or prudent under law.
  • Report to appropriate authorities and, where applicable, to recognized clearinghouses.

Examples (edit for your region):

  • United States: National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) CyberTipline — missingkids.org/gethelpnow/cybertipline — for U.S. providers as required by federal law when applicable.
  • European Union / UK: national law enforcement or INHOPE hotlines as required — inhope.org.
  • Other jurisdictions: competent national police or child-protection authorities.

Official requests (subpoenas, preservation, emergency disclosure) should be directed to legal@yourdomain.com with “URGENT — CHILD SAFETY” in the subject line when time-sensitive.

7. Data retention

We retain information no longer than necessary for safety, legal compliance, and dispute resolution. Reports involving CSAM may be retained longer where the law requires or where needed for active investigations. See our Privacy Policy URL for categories of data and retention.

8. No retaliation

We prohibit retaliation against anyone who reports suspected abuse in good faith. Bad-faith or false reports intended to harass may result in account action.
